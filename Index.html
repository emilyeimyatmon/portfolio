Group Project · Data Story

AI, Affordability, and Young Professionals in Oshawa

A human-centred data story exploring how the AI economy affects job prospects and housing affordability for emerging professionals in Oshawa.

AI Economy Affordability Human-Centred
  • AI-driven occupation shifts
  • Unemployment & job quality analysis
  • Rental affordability trends
  • StatsCan data (2021–2025)
Grounding Question

To what extent is AI affecting affordability for young professionals in Oshawa?

Introduction

Oshawa was affordable before AI. Now, rising rents, job disruption, and higher unemployment are making it harder to live here.

Problem Statement

AI is reshaping job opportunities in Canada. We take the top few affected fields in Canada to compare them in Ontario. Key issues: Rent is increasing in Oshawa and Unemployment rate is increasing.

Our Take on Solution

Data Flow: Industry affected by AI in Canada Chart (all industries) → Data wrapper map for Canada

Analysis: Take the top 5 industries affected in Canada and find the job market in Ontario for those top 5 affected industries → Number of People in each industry Chart (Ontario) → Data wrapper map for Ontario

Visualizations: Bar charts showing Average Automation Risk (%) vs. Industry (Education, Entertainment, Finance, Healthcare, Manufacturing, Retail, Transportation, etc.)

Personas

Persona 1 - Jamie: AI graduate struggling to find stable work and manage student debt.

Persona 2 - Christina: Part-time finance analyst who learned AI to upskill and improve her career prospects.

Persona 3 - Catherine: Data analyst using AI tools to upskill and push for promotion while still facing affordability challenges.

Interactive elements connect each persona to specific data points in the visualizations.

Conclusion

Use AI to develop your skills. The data story demonstrates that while AI disrupts traditional job markets, those who adapt and learn AI tools can navigate the changing landscape more successfully.

Format & Audience

Format: Scrollytelling explainer or browser-based interactive story

Target Audience: Government, universities, employers, banks, landlords, and graduating students.

Data Story

AI Impact on Affordability: A Timeline

Scroll through the timeline to see how AI-driven changes have affected rental affordability and unemployment rates for young professionals in Oshawa from 2021 to 2025.

Rental Affordability & Unemployment Trends (2021-2025)
2021
Pre-AI Impact Era
Oshawa was relatively affordable with average rent at $1,200/month. Unemployment rate at 6.2%. Young professionals could find stable employment and manage housing costs.
2022
Early AI Disruption
AI tools begin reshaping job markets. Rent increases to $1,350/month (+12.5%). Unemployment rises to 6.8% as traditional roles face automation pressure.
2023
Accelerated Change
Major industries adopt AI solutions. Rent jumps to $1,520/month (+26.7% from 2021). Unemployment reaches 7.5%. Young professionals face increased competition for AI-enhanced roles.
2024
Affordability Crisis
Rent reaches $1,680/month (+40% from 2021). Unemployment at 8.1%. Gap widens between those with AI skills and those without. Student debt becomes harder to manage.
2025
Current Reality
Rent at $1,850/month (+54% from 2021). Unemployment at 8.5%. Young professionals must adapt: upskilling in AI becomes essential for career survival and affordability.
